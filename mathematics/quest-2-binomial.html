Side Quest 2.1: Binomial Theorem

Expansion of (a+b)ⁿ

Historical Context

Blaise Pascal (17th century) - Developed Pascal's Triangle for binomial coefficients.

Isaac Newton (17th century) - Generalized binomial theorem to rational exponents.

Jacob Bernoulli (17th century) - Applied binomial theorem to probability theory.

Binomial Theorem

(a + b)ⁿ = Σ nCr aⁿ⁻ʳ bʳ

nCr = n! / [r!(n-r)!]

General term: Tᵣ₊₁ = nCr aⁿ⁻ʳ bʳ

Real-World Applications

Probability Theory - Binomial distribution models success/failure experiments
Statistics - Confidence intervals and hypothesis testing
Engineering - Approximation formulas and error analysis

Practice Exercise

Problem: Expand (2x + 3)⁴ using the binomial theorem.

Solution: Using binomial theorem:

(2x + 3)⁴ = Σ 4Cr (2x)⁴⁻ʳ (3)ʳ

= 4C0(2x)⁴ + 4C1(2x)³(3) + 4C2(2x)²(3)² + 4C3(2x)(3)³ + 4C4(3)⁴

= 16x⁴ + 4×8x³×3 + 6×4x²×9 + 4×2x×27 + 81

= 16x⁴ + 96x³ + 216x² + 216x + 81

Common Mistakes & Tips

Mistake: Confusing binomial coefficients with exponents

Tip: Remember nCr gives coefficients, exponents come from powers

Mistake: Forgetting to raise both terms to their powers

Tip: Always apply exponents to both a and b terms

Side Quest 2.2: Sequences & Series

Arithmetic and geometric

Sequences

Arithmetic: a, a+d, a+2d, ... | Tₙ = a + (n-1)d

Geometric: a, ar, ar², ... | Tₙ = arⁿ⁻¹

Sum of AP: Sₙ = n/2 [2a + (n-1)d]

Sum of GP: Sₙ = a(rⁿ-1)/(r-1) for r ≠ 1
