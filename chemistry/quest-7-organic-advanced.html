Side Quest 7.1: Reaction Mechanisms

Addition, substitution, elimination

Historical Context

Vladimir Markovnikov (1869) - Developed rule predicting addition across double bonds.

Alexander Zaitsev (1875) - Formulated rule predicting elimination product stability.

Christopher Ingold (1920s) - Developed modern mechanistic organic chemistry.

Reaction Types

Addition: Add across double bond ( Markovnikov)

Substitution: Replace atom/group (free radical)

Elimination: Remove to form double bond (Zaitsev)

Real-World Applications

Pharmaceutical Synthesis - Reaction mechanisms create complex drug molecules
Materials Science - Polymerization reactions produce plastics and fibers
Fine Chemicals - Industrial synthesis of fragrances and flavors

Practice Exercise

Problem: Predict the major product of HBr addition to propene using Markovnikov's rule.

Solution: Markovnikov's rule: H adds to less substituted carbon

Common Mistakes & Tips

Mistake: Confusing Markovnikov and anti-Markovnikov addition

Tip: Markovnikov: H to less substituted carbon, anti-Markovnikov: opposite

Mistake: Forgetting stereochemistry in reaction products

Tip: Always consider whether reaction creates chiral centers
